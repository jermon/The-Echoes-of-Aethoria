Beneath loose stones and hidden compartments, you discover items that have survived the ages: a journal bound in leather, still mostly intact; several small crystalline objects similar to but distinct from your artifact; and a map drawn on what appears to be treated silk, its markings faded but still readable. These are invaluable finds—evidence of the ancient civilization, its knowledge, and perhaps guidance for your own journey. The journal might contain the answers you seek about the artifact and the people who created it. The map could lead you to other sites of power or knowledge. These discoveries feel significant, as if you've found pieces of a larger puzzle. The question is whether you should take these treasures or leave them for others to find.
